1. Privacy Policy & Data Security

1.2 End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

How Encryption Works
When securing your account, you create a personal Encryption PIN. A cryptographic salt associated with your account may be stored securely on our servers. Your Encryption PIN, combined with this salt, is used locally on your device to derive an encryption key. Your encryption keys are generated and used on your device only.

Zero-Knowledge Design
Note You is designed so that we cannot access or read your decrypted notes. Your Encryption PIN is never intentionally transmitted to or stored on our servers. Because we do not possess your Encryption PIN or locally derived encryption keys, we are generally unable to recover or decrypt your note data.

Important Warning
If you forget your Encryption PIN, your encrypted data may become permanently inaccessible. Please store your PIN securely.

1.3 Incorrect PIN & Decryption Failures

Entering an incorrect Encryption PIN may generate an invalid encryption key, causing encrypted notes to fail decryption. As Note You does not store or verify your Encryption PIN, the app may not be able to determine whether the entered PIN is incorrect before attempting decryption.
